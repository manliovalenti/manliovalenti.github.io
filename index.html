Research interests

My research is focused on computable analysis, Weihrauch reducibility and effective descriptive set theory. My work explores the uniform computational strength of principles that, from the point of view of reverse mathematics, lie at the level of \( \mathrm{ATR}_0 \). I find the questions about the interactions between computability and classic analysis (e.g. measure theory or topology) particularly relevant.

In particular, I studied the open and clopen Ramsey theorems and the problem of finding descending sequences in an ill-founded linear order (and variants thereof). I also worked in algorithmic dimension theory, and, in particular, on characterizing the descriptive complexity of closed Salem sets, which are sets with equal Hausdorff and Fourier dimension.
